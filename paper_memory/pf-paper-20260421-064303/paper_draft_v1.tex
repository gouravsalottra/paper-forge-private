\section{Abstract}
This study evaluates passive concentration and momentum profitability in commodity futures using a pre-registered design. Statistical outputs include HAC t-tests, GARCH(1,1), bootstrap intervals, and regime checks. Key reported values include 1, 1, -0.006048834912895255, 0.00014199190829313372, 0.0, 1.0, 1.0, 14.793822321088985. The evidence is mixed and highlights uncertainty under Bonferroni adjustment.

\section{Data}
Data are daily futures returns for crude oil, gold, corn, natural gas, and copper over 2010-2023 from yfinance for this proof-of-concept run. Transformations use log returns and aligned trading dates. A signed DataPassport records hash, row count, and timestamp.

\section{Methodology}
# CODEC Pass 1
Data: yfinance commodity returns.
Methods: Sharpe, Newey-West HAC, GARCH(1,1), Markov regimes, Bonferroni, bootstrap.
Params: 252-day annualization, seeds [1337,42,9999].

\section{Results}
Primary outputs from stats tables indicate Newey-West p-value 0.4957 and adjusted threshold 0.0083, with zero Bonferroni-significant tests in this run. Sharpe outcomes vary by concentration and seed, including negative and positive realizations.

\section{Robustness}
Robustness checks include bootstrap confidence intervals, deflated Sharpe adjustment, GARCH persistence diagnostics, and Markov regime separation tests. Reported diagnostics suggest finite-sample instability and limited statistical power with nine scenario observations.

\section{Discussion}
The run demonstrates end-to-end protocol execution and artifact traceability. Empirical conclusions are constrained by sample size and simulation budget relative to the full PAP target. A larger 500,000-episode execution is required for final inference strength.

\section{Conclusion}
The proof-of-concept confirms that the pipeline can produce locked, auditable research artifacts from specification through draft and review. Current estimates are directionally informative but not conclusive under multiple-testing correction.
